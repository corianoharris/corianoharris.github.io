How I integrate principles and tools.

What I apply to everything I do.

I use SOLID principles (like Single Responsibility and Dependency Inversion) and the DRY principle (Don’t Repeat Yourself) to keep my code neat and easy to maintain. I stick to the simplest tools that work best, thanks to the Rule of Least Power. When designing, I make things intuitive and engaging by focusing on hierarchy, balance, and contrast. I approach problems with a systematic design process, breaking them down into manageable chunks with modular and component-driven methods. For making decisions, I use RAPID principles (Recommend, Agree, Perform, Input, Decide) and the Arrange-Act-Assert (AAA) pattern for testing. I also dive into data to pull out the key info needed to make decisions. Empathy and accessibility guide everything I do, ensuring that solutions are both inclusive and user-friendly. Clear communication, mentoring, and a collaborative approach are important to me. My problem-solving style is creative and flexible, always aiming to find the best solutions.
